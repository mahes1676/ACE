What We Do

Club Activities

Regular structured sessions that keep every member growing — from weekly concept labs to cross-domain project building and campus-wide awareness drives.

All Wings

Weekly Learning Sessions

45–60 min concept sessions followed by hands-on lab exercises. Structured, consistent, and practical across all three wings every week.

Cybersecurity

CTF Competitions

Monthly internal CTFs plus semester-based external participation on Hack The Box, TryHackMe, and picoCTF. Compete, solve, learn.

Cybersecurity

Security Labs

Hands-on labs in web security, network analysis, and Linux exploitation using Kali Linux, virtual machines, and isolated lab environments.

AI

AI Model Building

Build, train, and evaluate ML/DL models from scratch. Kaggle competitions, Google Colab labs, and project showcases every semester.

Embedded

Hardware Project Nights

Prototype IoT devices, program microcontrollers, and build real embedded systems — from sensor arrays to secure hardware demos.

Cross-Wing

Interdisciplinary Projects

Collaborative builds across wings — intelligent threat detection systems, AI-secured IoT devices, network intrusion detectors. Real impact.

Campus

Awareness Campaigns

Campus sessions on phishing, social media safety, digital privacy, and AI ethics. Sharing knowledge beyond the club to the whole college.

All Wings

Guest Lectures & Research Talks

Invite cybersecurity professionals, AI researchers, and embedded engineers. Members also present their own research and project findings.

Hackathons

Internal Hackathons

Semester-end internal hackathons with cross-wing teams. Build something in 24 hours that solves a real problem — judged by faculty and peers.
